Poor digital communication can cause significant problems. For instance, a student once sent a group email with incorrect meeting details for a project submission. As a result, several group members missed the meeting, causing delays in completing the project and affecting their grades. This issue could have been avoided by double-checking the email content before sending, using clear subject lines, and confirming receipt with group members. Additionally, including all relevant information such as date, time, and platform ensures clarity. Effective digital communication, such as professional emails, instant messaging etiquette, and organized documentation, is crucial in academic and professional settings. Practicing careful and clear communication prevents misunderstandings, builds trust, and ensures tasks are completed efficiently and on time.
