# Case Study: Phishing Attack

**Cybercrime Type:** Phishing  

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by impersonating legitimate institutions.  

**How It Happens (Step-by-Step):**  
1. The victim receives a seemingly legitimate email, SMS, or WhatsApp message, often appearing to come from a bank, payment app, or popular website.  
2. The message contains a link to a fake website that looks identical to the official one.  
3. The victim clicks the link and is prompted to enter login credentials, UPI PIN, or other personal information.  
4. The attacker collects the submitted data and uses it to access bank accounts, steal money, or commit identity fraud.  

**Targeted Victims:**  
Phishing attacks often target college students, working professionals, or anyone unfamiliar with digital security, especially those who frequently use online banking, UPI apps, or social media platforms.  

**Consequences:**  
Victims may lose money, have their bank accounts drained, or suffer identity theft. Recovery can be lengthy, and emotional stress is common. Organizations may also face reputational damage if users’ information is compromised.

Fictional scenario inspired by real incidents reported on the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in).
